Neuroscience PhD candidate · MEG-Center, University of Tübingen · CherISH (MSCA) Doctoral Network

“I am a PhD candidate in Neuroscience at the University of Tübingen (MEG-Center) and a Marie Skłodowska-Curie fellow in the CherISH network. My research develops audio-tactile systems and training paradigms to improve spatial hearing in cochlear-implant users, combining EEG/MEG, multi-speaker arrays, and deep neural networks to design rehabilitation strategies for real-world listening.”

Research

Audio-tactile training for CI users

Structured Spatial Training (SST) and Ecological Spatial Training (EST) with 7-speaker arrays, EEG markers (MMN, MMRS), and adaptive feedback.

DNN sound localization

In-ear microphones → time-frequency features → direction decoding across head poses and rooms; mapped to body-centered tactile cues.
